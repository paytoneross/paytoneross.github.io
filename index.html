First Individual Game

       This was the first project that I worked on in the program. I was just starting working in Unity and in Animate, as you can see from my state of the art animation and shading. We had to chose one of three 2D stub games, a platformer, tower defense, or top down shooter, and make our own game out of them. I chose the platformer, and I decided to create a game that combines platforming challenges with metroidvania-like exploration and progression. The player explores the interior of a scary haunted pyramid, and has find movement upgrades in order to survive and escape.

       My initial idea for the game was much grander than I ended up being able to make, but I'm still proud of what I came up with. The biggest things I took from this game were learning how to work in sprints, avoiding out-scoping myself, and what makes a small game punch above its weight class. When I look back on this game now, all that I see is everything that is wrong with it and how much better I could do it now, which I think that shows how far I've come. Below is a trailer that I made for this game in my current Interactive Audio/Video class.

Interactive Comic

       In this project, we were tasked with creating a comic that explained a complex quantum science principle. For our group, it was the bi-directional flow of time. Given how complex our topic is, we decided to make a story where we apply this concept to a character narrative and show the effects that this theory could have on someone. We thought a lot about how to create a powerful story that displays this topic in a way that the average person can understand and sympathize with. My contributions to this project were 4 comic panels, narrative/dialogue writing, Unity implementation, and development of the mini game.

       The class we were in was all about visual storytelling, the ways that people percieve information, and methods to subtly convey meaning without people realizing. One of the ways I applied these concepts to my work was by making a mini auto runner game where the player runs to the left instead of the right like they normally do in the genre. Combining this with a playspace that evolves as you progress helps to create an experience were everything comes together to make you feel like you are travelling backwards in time. Below are two videos, one of me explaining the project and my contributions in more detail, and one of a full playthrough of our comic.

AR Group Project

       This was the second game I developed in my first semester, and the first group game project I worked on in the program. The assignment was to make an AR game experience that could be played on a mobile device. After a few brainstorming sessions, we decided on an FPS where you would spawn a castle using plane detection and once you do a bunch of tiny guys start shooting at you. The goal is to clear them all out while dodging their shots by moving around. My contribution to this project was assisting with AR implementation, the design of the castle, the shooting mechanics, touch screen controls, and UI/UX and HUD design.

       We only had a few weeks to put this project together, and I'm very proud of what we made and how much we learned in such a short amount of time. My biggest takeaways from this project was the value of working on projects as a team. They say teams are stronger than heroes, and that was proven to me when we worked on this project.
